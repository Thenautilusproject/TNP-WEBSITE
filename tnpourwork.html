Our Work

To deliver our service of conservation we focus on three key areas: Youth Education, Marine Research and Environmental Protection   

Youth Education

Classroom sessions

We are committed to teaching children about our coastline and the animals that reside along it through classrom sessions. Topics include: Marine Invertebrates, Marine Vertebrates, Turtles of the Mediterranean, Dangers of Plastic Pollution, Climate Change in our Oceans, Entering a microscopic world and Sharks vs Dolphins. These courses can be tailored in length and content to meet the exact national curriculum requirements of any institution. Available from years 2 – 13 (academic years).

Field Trips

One of our most popular offerings is the Coast Crawler Field Trip where children learn about the marine invertebrates along the coastline. Children get a full interactive experience in our Capture, Learn and Relase (CLR) programme, where they begin to understand the habitats and feeding behaviours of these amazing creatures.

Survey Sessions

Focused on collecting data for science GCSE or A’Level course practical requirements. We have a number of custom projects which reliably meet the demands of practical work. If you are interested in booking a survey session then please Contact Us soonest to avoid disappointment

Marine Research

Field Research

We are currently undertaking a large variety of field research. This includes closely monitoring the coastline populations of the European spider crab, triton snail and our incredible long and short snouted seahorses. This is undertaken through a combination of SCUBA and snorkeling, where the animals are identified and important information such as size, weight and sex are recorded. In conjunction, we have stepped into the microscopic world, with one of our leading members, Lewis Stagnetto, currently conducting a PhD into the phytoplankton population structure in the Straits of Gibraltar.

Lab Research

At the present moment we are in the process of setting up a laboratory premises. Here, we hope to conduct more targeted research on our marine species which will directly contribute to the wealth of marine knowledge locally and beyond.

Environment Protection

Beach Clean-Ups

We run a large number of beach clean-ups throughout the year to help remove unwanted plastic and litter from our coastline. These often occur in conjunction with local companies, who engage their employees in this environmental practice. Coastal waters clean-ups are also undertaken in which our team take to the sea often by snorkeling or SCUBA to remove debris from the sea bed.

Sustainability Awards

Every year we host The Nautilus Sustainability Awards, in which organisations are carefully selected for their outstanding green credentials. Throughout the year smaller awards are given to businesses which are reforming their practices in a green manner. This includes the #Plastic2Paper and #PawsOffStraws initiatives.

Animal Protection

Much of our marine research is conducive towards attaining a good understanding on the population dynamics of Girbatlar’s vulnerable species. With the requisite knowledge, we can begin to implement measures of habitat protection and species conservation, further gathering the support of our local authorities.
