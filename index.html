The Nautilus Project

Protecting Gibraltar’s coastline since 2016. We research and educate in the hope of inspiring future generations to become samaritans of the sea.

About Us

Our Mission

Marine Conservation

As a registered charity, our team of volunteers work tirelessly to ensure that our coastline is clean, healthy and plastic free. Through beach cleans, sustainability awards and school outreach programmes we engage our community into conservation practices. This is supplemented with an abundance of marine research, which gives us new and improved insights into the dynamics of our marine ecosystem.

Marine Research

It is our social duty to monitor our marine species. Public sightings and recordings are collated through our NEMO app and supplemental data is collected by our experienced dive team to closely monitor more vulnerable species, such as our short and long snouted seahorses.

Youth Education

Key to our work is to inspire future generations to become Samaritans of the sea. Our approach is active and hands on, where children can participate in our coastal crawler school field trips or regular snorkeling excursions. We believe that with marine life, to see is to inspire.


NEMO

NEMO is our user friendly application which employs a community approach to marine research. In just a few taps, users can log animal sightings, from whales to jellyfish, on-the-go.

Automatic timestamps and GPS coordinates from the sightings are sent through to our NEMO database, from which we assess each and every individual data log for possible errors. These data logs are then collated to form important data sets that allow us to interpret the animals behaviour, from feeding times to migrations patterns. For example, our data shows that the Portugese man o’ war entered our bay within a day's difference between two consecutive years. Swimmers can now be warned as to when to keep their eyes out for these stinging gliders.
