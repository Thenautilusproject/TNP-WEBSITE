NEMO

NEMO is our user friendly application which employs a community approach to marine research. In just a few taps, users can log animal sightings, from whales to jellyfish, on-the-go.

Automatic timestamps and GPS coordinates from the sightings are sent through to our NEMO database, from which we assess each and every individual data log for possible errors. These data logs are then collated to form important data sets that allow us to interpret the animals behaviour, from feeding times to migrations patterns. For example, our data shows that the Portugese man o’ war entered our bay within a day's difference between two consecutive years. Swimmers can now be warned as to when to keep their eyes out for these stinging gliders.

How to Use NEMO

  • Download

  • Head on over to the iOS or Android app store and search for the NEMO app by Lewis Stagnetto. Download the app onto your mobile device. Once complete, open the app and create your very own user login.

  • Add Sighting

  • When you are out and about, and happen to see an animal of interest, open up the app and begin the short and simple process of documenting it. This can be anything from a crab to a dolphin - every little bit of data helps! Start the process by selecting the location of the sighting. Your best estimate works.

  • Select Species

  • When navigating the app and having selected the animal type which you have sighted, you can then progress to selecting the species.

  • Submit

  • Prior to submitting you can add any additional images or descriptions to your sighting. If unsure of the species, this will allow our team to take a second look to make sure all our content is as accurate as possible. Once all the information has be inputed, simply hit the submit button and see your animal location arrow appear on our NEMO maps.
